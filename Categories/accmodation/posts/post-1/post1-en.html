Step-by-step Procedures:

What to prepare before renting?

  • local phone no
  • local bank
  • sufficient amount for at least 1 year rent
  • passport
  • study/work permits
  • letter of school acceptance
  • financial proof

What to consider before renting?

  • Location - Transportation & Groceries
  • Parking
  • Basic appliances: Dishwasher, oven, microwave, fridge, laundry suite(wash and dry)
  • Budget
    • Rent
      → at least 1 year tenancy period
      → 1-year rent deposit mostly for student; After 1 year, rent is submitted every month instead
    • Utilities fee (Water ~$60-70/month, Heating ~$50-60/month, Hydro ~$70-80/month)
    • Utilities account set-up fee $25 (one-time)
    • Tenant insurance $30-40/month
    • Internet $60-80/month
    • Key deposit $300-$500 (one time)
  • Amenities (gym room, swimming pool etc.)
  • Pet/ Smoking allowed?
  • Age of Building
    • too new: amenities and some appliances may not be available, construction noise
    • too old: many dust, some appliances may require maintenance

How to rent

Suggeseted website:

  • Conda.ca (with agent)
  • Rentals.ca
  • Realtor.ca

Steps:

  1. Save all buildings you want visit
  2. Contact agent to book a viewing (need 24hrs before to notice the current tenant)
    *Must book for viewing, risks:
    • All appliances available? working?
    • The layout of room (1 bedroom vs studio)
    • The cleanliness (for old-age building)
    • The lighting (maybe quite dark)
  3. Make an offer
    • Fill in a Rental Application Form for the Agent to draft an offer
      (Personal information, Past working history- financial status, let the landlord to have a picture of your credibility)
    • Agent drafted an offer with 4 forms to let you sign,
      1. Agreement to lease (basic info)
      2. Confirmation of co-operation and Representation (listing brokerage)
      3. Schedule - Agreement to lease (**agreement)
      4. Residential Tenancy Agreement ($)

      Double check:

      • Personal info, working history
      • Rent Address, Tenancy period
      • $$: Rent, Deposit, Utilities, Internet, Tenant insurance, Key deposit (which are included)
      • Agreement: landlord & tenant agree xxx
      • Brokerage: release rent to the landlord every month? or every year?

      ***Please read through the contract

      • Some wordings in contract may different from what you've discussed with the agent
        because the agent usually say things briefly, so please ask the agent if anything uncertain.
      • The agent might have typos.

      *** The forms you have signed = A Legal contract (once the landlord accepted)

      → Don't make more than 1 offer at the same time, so think it carefully before making an offer


      Real experience: We've visited a very new condo which the unit didn't install the fridge yet.
      We still signed the contract and paid the rent since the agent said it must be installed within 2-3 days after we checked in.
      However, the landload said it's not guaranteed, so we just have to accepted it... Otherwise we have to go to the law or achieve mutual consent to cancel the contract (also have to wait for refund).

  4. Wait for Landlord accept your offer
    • Landlord have 24 hours to consider
    • May reject your offer, because they may receive some other offers, the landlord would chose among them


      • Real experience: The Landload verbally agreed to rent the unit to us on the phone since we're both from Hong Kong.
      However, they suddenly rejected us in the another morning without any reasons lol.

  5. Offer accepted
    • Landlord signed the documents
    • May have some additional statements by the landlord, read the whole contract carefully again & resign again
  6. Submit deposit within 24hours after offer accepted

    If your bank is different from the brokerage’s:
    A) make a wire transfer ($15) (electronic $ transfer between banks)
    b) get a Bank draft (~$10)


    If your bank is same as the brokerage’s
    Go to your bank branch to make a Direct deposit ($0)
    Rmb to ask for the deposit slip to take picture (the bank will keep it)

  7. Offer Secured

    proof:

    1. Final lease document (4 Forms signed combined)
    2. Brokerage receipt

  8. Before getting keys:
    1. Pay Key Deposit to Landlord
      • E transfer with email
      • screenshot transaction
    2. Set up Utilities account
      • Download the confirmation pdf
    3. Tenant Insurance

      Suggested insurance: Square one insurance

      • Depends on your needs to choose how much to be covered
      • Add your together-living friends/family name at last few steps
        (bf/gf/friend is identified as family member when you are filling in the box of who will you live with)
      • Download the confirmation pdf

      *Send the above 3 confirmation documents to agent, agent will send to landlord and brokerage

    4. Registration & Move-in elevator booking with the Condo Management
      • Registration Form
      • Passport photos
      • Final lease document
    5. Date with landlord and management company for the Key pickup time (through your agent)
    6. Set up Internet

      Suggested company: Bell

      • Back to school offer: $60/month
      • make a booking for setting up home internet
  9. First Day of Moving In, CHECK:
    • all appliances working or not
    • anythings broken?
    • any damage to floor, walls, doors, ceiling?

    → take pictures and report to the landlord immediately to protect yourself from compensation

  10. DONE
